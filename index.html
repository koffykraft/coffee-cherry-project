Coffee Experience Landscape · Working Structure

How Coffee Becomes Experience

A working structure for exploring coffee as plant, fruit, seed, beverage, aroma, flavour, memory, and experience within the wider Buna Library.

This landscape does not begin with tasting notes. It begins with a simple question: how does a coffee become an experience? The goal is not to memorise descriptors. The goal is to understand where they come from, why they differ, and how humans learn to recognise them.

Working Discipline

Observe first. Describe later. Let the cup speak before the label does.

Working Assumption

Different people may experience the same coffee differently. That is not failure. It is part of perception.

The Elements of Experience

A cup can be opened into smaller experiences: aroma, taste, flavour, texture, temperature, acidity, sweetness, and bitterness.

  1. AromaWhat reaches the nose before and during drinking.
  2. TasteSweet, sour, bitter, salty, umami — and what they are not.
  3. FlavorThe combined experience of aroma, taste, mouthfeel, and memory.
  4. Body and MouthfeelWeight, texture, smoothness, dryness, and astringency.
  5. AcidityBrightness, sharpness, fruit impression, structure, and sourness.
  6. SweetnessPerceived sweetness, actual sugars, aroma association, and balance.
  7. BitternessNot always a defect. Sometimes structure, sometimes harshness.
  8. TemperatureHow the same cup changes as it cools.

  7. StorageGreen coffee as a living-ish material still changing with time.

Part VII

The Sensory Atlas

Descriptor territories are not exams. They are aids to noticing. Each page will ask what people may mean, where the impression may come from, and how to learn it gently.

  1. FloralJasmine, rose, orange blossom, tea-like perfume.
  2. FruitThe broadest territory: fresh, cooked, dried, jammy, wine-like.
  3. CitrusLemon, lime, orange, grapefruit, peel, zest, acidity and aroma.
  4. BerryBlueberry, blackberry, strawberry, blackcurrant, red fruit.
  5. Stone FruitPeach, apricot, plum, nectarine.
  6. Tropical FruitMango, pineapple, banana, passion fruit, jackfruit-like associations.
  7. Dried FruitRaisin, date, fig, prune, dried apricot, concentration and darker sweetness.
  8. NutAlmond, hazelnut, peanut, walnut, roasted seed impressions.
  9. ChocolateCocoa, dark chocolate, milk chocolate, cacao nib, roast sweetness.
  10. CaramelCaramelised sugar, jaggery, toffee, molasses, brown sweetness.
  11. SpiceClove, cinnamon, pepper, cardamom-like warmth and aromatic lift.
  12. HerbalGreen, grassy, leafy, tea-like, fresh plant impressions.
  13. EarthSoil, wood, humus, mushroom, forest floor, mineral associations.
  14. FermentedWiney, boozy, vinegar, lactic, ripe fruit, controlled and uncontrolled edges.
  15. RoastToast, smoke, char, ash, roasted nuts, roasted coffee identity.
