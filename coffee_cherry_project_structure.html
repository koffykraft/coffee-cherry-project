Coffee Cherry Project · Working Structure

From Plant to Cherry

A first structure for testing whether the Buna / Citane method can travel without forcing Citane’s old architecture onto coffee cherries.

This project should not begin with processing methods. It should begin with the plant, its movement through culture, and the older human uses that existed before the roasted bean became the centre of the story.

Working Discipline

Observe first. Let explanations arrive later. Allow each structure to earn its place.

Transfer Test

The goal is not to transfer Citane. The goal is to discover what lay underneath Citane.

Part I

Meeting the Plant

Before the cherry, there is the coffee plant. Not a bean. Not a beverage. A living plant that entered human attention in more than one form.

  1. The Coffee PlantCoffee as shrub, forest plant, flowering perennial, fruiting body, seed-bearing plant.
  2. Where Coffee Comes FromEthiopian landscapes, Yemen, trade routes, gardens, estates, colonies, and new coffee geographies.
  3. Coffee Before CoffeeOlder uses of the plant: fruit, leaves, husk, decoctions, food, medicine, ritual, fodder, fuel, compost.
  4. Culture Around the PlantHow people noticed, prepared, valued, traded, argued over, and ritualised coffee before modern specialty language.
Part II

Meeting the Cherry

The cherry should arrive as one expression of the plant, not as an isolated processing object.

  1. The Landscape of the Coffee CherryThe first page already drafted: a fruit on a branch, and the many paths that begin once it is picked.
  2. RipenessColour, firmness, sweetness, uneven maturity, overripe fruit, underripe fruit, fallen fruit.
  3. PickingThe first human decision: what to take, what to leave, what to sort, what to accept.
  4. Life After HarvestA picked cherry is no longer on the tree, but it continues to change.
Part III

Guiding Change

Leave these as headers for now. Do not overbuild them until the observations demand structure.

  1. A Fruit That Continues to ChangeObservation before processing terminology.
  2. Different Ways People Guided That ChangeWashed, natural, honey, pulped natural, anaerobic and other methods can appear here later.
  3. Drying as DecisionSlow, fast, raised bed, patio, shade, sun, thickness, turning, stopping point.
  4. Fermentation as NoticingSmell, texture, time, risk, control, tradition, measurement.
Part IV

The Seed Becomes Coffee

The bean enters only after the fruit has been understood.

  1. The Seed Inside the FruitBean as seed, not starting point.
  2. Resting, Storage, and TimeWhat changes after processing appears complete.
  3. Roasting the SeedOnly later: heat, colour, aroma, development.
  4. Brewing the Memory of the FruitThe cup as the final place where earlier decisions may become visible.

Emerging Structure

Citane path: Plant → Leaf → Transformation → Beverage.

Coffee cherry path: Plant → Culture → Fruit → Change → Seed → Beverage.

This is not a final taxonomy. It is a working scaffold. If the cherry produces a better structure, this one should be revised or abandoned.
