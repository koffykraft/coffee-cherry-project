Drying

Drying is not just removing water. It is one of the most important stages of preserving coffee.

After processing, coffee is still vulnerable. If drying is too fast, too slow, uneven, careless, or stopped at the wrong point, earlier good work can be lost.
Part I

Why Drying Matters

Coffee must be dried before it can rest, store, travel, and roast.

Drying reduces moisture and slows the changes that could damage the seed.

  1. StabilityDrying helps make coffee safer to store and transport.
  2. PreservationGood drying protects work already done in farming and processing.
  3. Risk ControlPoor drying can lead to mould, off-flavours, fading, or uneven roasting.
Part II

Drying Is A Process

Drying is not one moment. It happens over hours or days, depending on method, weather, thickness, airflow, and coffee type.

  1. Surface MoistureWater on the outside leaves first.
  2. Internal MoistureMoisture inside the seed moves more slowly.
  3. AirflowMoving air helps remove moisture.
  4. TurningRegular movement helps reduce uneven drying.
Part III

Common Drying Surfaces

Different drying systems create different levels of control.

The method matters, but management matters more.

  1. PatioCoffee is spread on a hard surface and turned regularly.
  2. Raised BedsAir can move above and below the coffee.
  3. Shade DryingSlower drying with protection from direct sun.
  4. Mechanical DryingUseful when weather is difficult, but must be managed carefully.
Part IV

Too Fast, Too Slow, Uneven

Drying problems often come from speed and unevenness. The goal is not simply to finish quickly. The goal is to dry well.

  1. Too FastCan stress the seed and produce uneven internal moisture.
  2. Too SlowCan allow mould, fermentation, or stale impressions to develop.
  3. Uneven DryingCreates mixed material that may roast and age unpredictably.
  4. Weather PressureRain, humidity, heat, and cloud cover can change drying decisions daily.
Part V

What Reaches The Cup

Good drying may not announce itself.

Poor drying often does. It can appear later as mustiness, flatness, harshness, bagginess, mould, or a tired cup.

  1. CleanlinessCareful drying helps preserve clean expression.
  2. Shelf LifeWell-dried coffee usually stores more reliably.
  3. Roasting BehaviourUneven moisture can make roasting harder to control.
  4. Final QualityDrying cannot create good coffee alone, but it can protect or damage it.

Page Direction

Drying is preservation with risk.

It is quiet work, but it decides whether good fruit and good processing survive long enough to become good coffee.
