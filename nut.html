Nut

Nut notes often sit between sweetness, roast, and texture.

Nut does not mean the coffee contains nuts. It means the cup suggests almond, hazelnut, peanut, walnut, roasted seed, or similar impressions through aroma, sweetness, bitterness, and roast development.
Part I

The Shape Of Nut

Nutty impressions are usually warm, rounded, and dry rather than juicy.

They may feel sweet, roasted, creamy, bitter, woody, or seed-like depending on the coffee and roast.

  1. WarmthNut notes often feel gentle and rounded.
  2. Roast LinkSome nut impressions become clearer with roasting.
  3. DrynessNut character may feel less juicy than fruit character.
  4. TextureNut notes may sit close to body and mouthfeel.
Part II

Common Nut Directions

Nut words are useful when they point to a clear sensory direction. They should not be used as a default word for any mild coffee.

  1. AlmondSoft, sweet, pale, and sometimes delicate.
  2. HazelnutRounded, sweet, aromatic, and often linked with chocolate impressions.
  3. PeanutRoasted, familiar, sometimes dry or savoury.
  4. WalnutDarker, more bitter, woody, or slightly tannic.
  5. Roasted SeedSesame, sunflower, pumpkin seed, or general toasted-seed character.
Part III

Raw, Toasted, Roasted

Nut character changes greatly with heat.

Raw almond, toasted sesame, roasted peanut, and dark walnut are different sensory ideas.

  1. RawPale, mild, soft, sometimes green or undeveloped.
  2. ToastedWarm, aromatic, sweet, and light brown.
  3. RoastedDeeper, drier, darker, and more roast-linked.
  4. BitterSome nut impressions carry pleasant or unpleasant bitterness.
Part IV

Where Nut May Come From

Nutty character may be shaped by coffee material, roast development, extraction, and the way sweetness and bitterness meet.

  1. RoastingMany nut-like aromas appear or become visible through heat.
  2. DevelopmentRoast choices can make nut notes soft, sweet, dry, or harsh.
  3. ProcessingCleaner processes may leave nut and cocoa impressions more readable.
  4. BrewingExtraction can make nut notes round, hollow, bitter, or balanced.
Part V

A Professional Reading

Do not use nut as a vague safe word.

Ask whether the impression is pale or dark, sweet or bitter, raw or roasted, clean or stale.

  1. Is It Sweet?Almond or hazelnut may be closer.
  2. Is It Roasted?Peanut, toasted seed, or roasted nut may be closer.
  3. Is It Bitter?Walnut, dark roast, or extraction may be involved.
  4. Is It Fresh?Nutty notes should not drift into stale, rancid, or papery impressions.

Page Direction

Nut is a warm, dry, roast-linked family.

It helps the taster recognise the movement from fruit and floral expression toward sweetness, cocoa, roast, texture, and balance.
