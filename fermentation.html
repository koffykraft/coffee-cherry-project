Fermentation

Fermentation is not new to coffee. Control is the newer conversation.

Whenever coffee fruit, mucilage, water, microbes, time, and warmth meet, fermentation can begin. The professional question is not only whether fermentation happened. It is how it was managed.
Part I

What Fermentation Means Here

Fermentation is microbial activity.

Yeasts and bacteria act on sugars and other available material in the fruit and mucilage. This changes the material around the seed.

  1. MicrobesYeasts, bacteria, and other organisms become active under suitable conditions.
  2. SubstrateFruit pulp and mucilage provide sugars and other material.
  3. TimeThe longer conditions allow activity, the further change can move.
  4. TemperatureWarmth can speed activity. Cooler conditions can slow it.
Part II

Fermentation Was Always Present

Coffee did not wait for modern language before fermenting. Traditional washed, natural, and honey processes all involve microbial activity in different ways.

  1. Washed CoffeeFermentation may help loosen mucilage before washing.
  2. Natural CoffeeThe whole cherry dries with fruit material still around the seed.
  3. Honey CoffeeMucilage remains during drying, allowing another kind of contact.
  4. Uncontrolled ChangeIf poorly managed, fermentation can move into rot, mould, or harsh off-flavours.
Part III

Modern Control

The modern discussion is less about discovering fermentation.

It is about controlling it more deliberately.

  1. Time ControlHow long coffee remains in a tank, bag, barrel, cherry, or mucilage contact.
  2. Oxygen ControlOpen, sealed, low-oxygen, or anaerobic-style conditions.
  3. Temperature ControlManaging heat build-up or slowing activity.
  4. Microbial ControlUsing selected cultures or encouraging particular microbial pathways.
Part IV

What Fermentation Can Influence

Fermentation can influence aroma, acidity, sweetness impression, body, and aftertaste. It can also create defects. The difference is often control.

  1. AromaCan move toward fruit, wine, spice, floral, or fermented notes.
  2. AcidityCan feel cleaner, sharper, softer, lactic, acetic, or unstable.
  3. BodyCan feel heavier, rounder, or more textured.
  4. RiskExcess or poor control can produce vinegar, rot, mould, solvent, or dirty flavours.
Part V

Do Not Confuse Intensity With Quality

A strongly fermented coffee is not automatically better.

A quiet coffee is not automatically simple. A professional looks for clarity, balance, cleanliness, intention, and stability.

  1. Is It Clean?Does the fermentation character sit clearly, or does it feel muddy?
  2. Is It Balanced?Does fruit, acidity, body, and sweetness hold together?
  3. Is It Stable?Does the cup remain pleasant as it cools?
  4. Is It Suitable?Does the method serve the coffee, or only announce itself?

Page Direction

Fermentation is guided microbial change.

It is useful when it improves the expression of the coffee. It is a problem when it overwhelms the material or hides poor handling.
