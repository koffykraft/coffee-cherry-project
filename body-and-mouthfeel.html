Body and Mouthfeel

Some parts of coffee are felt more than tasted.

Body is not flavour. It is the weight, texture, and physical presence of the drink in the mouth.
Part I

The Weight of the Cup

A coffee can feel light like tea.

Another can feel round, thick, creamy, or heavy.

This is body.

  1. LightDelicate, tea-like, quick, clean.
  2. MediumPresent but not heavy. Often balanced and easy to drink.
  3. HeavyDense, coating, bold, sometimes syrup-like.
Part II

Texture

Texture is how the liquid moves and rests in the mouth.

  1. SilkySmooth and flowing.
  2. CreamySoft, round, and fuller.
  3. SyrupyThick, sweet-feeling, slow-moving.
  4. ThinLight, watery, or lacking presence.
Part III

Dryness and Astringency

Not all mouthfeel is smooth.

Some coffees leave the mouth feeling dry, rough, tight, or chalky.

  1. DryingA feeling of moisture being pulled from the mouth.
  2. AstringentA firmer drying sensation, often felt on the tongue, gums, or cheeks.
  3. ChalkyPowdery or dusty in the finish.
Part IV

What Changes Mouthfeel?

Mouthfeel is shaped by the coffee and by preparation.

  1. Brewing MethodEspresso, filter, immersion, and milk drinks create different bodies.
  2. ExtractionToo little can feel thin. Too much can feel dry or rough.
  3. RoastRoast level can change weight, bitterness, smoothness, and dryness.
  4. MilkMilk adds fat, softness, sweetness, and coating texture.
Part V

A Simple Comparison

Compare water, black tea, milk, and honey.

Do not think about flavour first. Notice weight. Notice movement. Notice coating.

  1. WaterThin, clean, quick.
  2. TeaLight, sometimes drying.
  3. MilkRounder and softer.
  4. HoneyThick, slow, coating.

Page Direction

Mouthfeel gives coffee physical shape.

Before asking what a coffee tastes like, ask how it feels in the mouth.
