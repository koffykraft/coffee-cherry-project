Citane

Most people encounter coffee through the seed. Citane begins elsewhere.

Citane begins with the coffee leaf. Not because the seed has become unimportant, and not as a replacement for roasted coffee. It begins with the leaf because the coffee plant contains more than one possible story.
Part I

What Citane Begins With

Citane begins with the coffee leaf, not with roasted seed coffee. That difference matters because the leaf is not an unfinished bean. It is a different material from the same plant.

  1. LeafThe primary material is the leaf of the coffee plant.
  2. SelectionAge, position, health, shade, season, and handling can all matter.
  3. ProcessingWithering, bruising, drying, heating, oxidation, or other steps may shape the result.
  4. PreparationInfusion and decoction create different expressions.
Part II

What Citane Is Not

The clearest way to protect the idea is to avoid false claims.

  1. Not Coffee SubstituteIt does not need to imitate roasted coffee.
  2. Not Tea CopyIt may borrow preparation ideas, but it should not be reduced to tea language.
  3. Not Wellness TheatreAny functional claims must remain cautious and evidence-led.
  4. Not A ShortcutThe leaf requires its own observation, processing, and sensory discipline.
Part III

What Citane May Become

Citane can grow as a beverage, an ingredient, and a method of study. The point is not to decide too quickly, but to observe carefully enough that the category can form honestly.

  1. BeverageA prepared drink from coffee leaves.
  2. IngredientA possible base for culinary use, blending, or extraction.
  3. Research SubjectA way to ask better questions about coffee leaves.
  4. Farm ExpressionA way to make more of the coffee plant visible.
Part IV

Why Buna Holds Citane

Buna is the larger frame because it can hold seed, fruit, leaf, flower, tradition, preparation, research, and experience without forcing them into one product.

  1. Plant FirstThe coffee plant is larger than the roasted seed.
  2. Multiple PathsSeed, fruit, and leaf can each become different experiences.
  3. Respect For DifferenceCitane does not need to taste like coffee to belong to coffee.
  4. Careful ExpansionThe category should grow through observation, not exaggeration.
Part V

A Professional Reading

Citane should be approached with curiosity and restraint. It is promising because it is unfinished, not because it is already explained.

  1. Observe FirstLet the leaf and cup speak before applying labels.
  2. Document CarefullyLeaf source, processing, brewing, and sensory response should be recorded.
  3. Compare HonestlyCompare batches without forcing conclusions.
  4. Build SlowlyA durable category grows through patient work.

Page Direction

Citane is the coffee leaf made visible.

It closes this landscape by returning to the plant and opening a next question: what becomes possible when we stop treating the roasted seed as the whole coffee story?
