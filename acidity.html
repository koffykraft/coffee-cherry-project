Acidity

Acidity is not the same as sourness.

In coffee, acidity can mean brightness, lift, fruit, freshness, structure, or sharpness. It depends on balance.
Part I

The First Misunderstanding

Many people hear acidity and think sour.

Sometimes coffee is sour. But acidity can also be pleasant. It can make a cup feel alive.

  1. BrightLively, clear, lifted.
  2. FreshFruit-like, clean, open.
  3. SharpPointed, aggressive, sometimes unpleasant.
Part II

Acidity Gives Shape

Without acidity, coffee can feel flat. Too much acidity can feel thin or biting.

  1. Low AciditySoft, round, calm, sometimes dull.
  2. Medium AcidityBalanced, lively, easy to drink.
  3. High AcidityBright, sparkling, fruit-like, or sharp depending on balance.
Part III

Fruit Helps Explain It

Think of fruit.

A ripe orange, a green apple, a lemon, and a grape can all be acidic. They do not feel the same.

  1. LemonSharp, direct, high.
  2. OrangeBright, sweet, rounded.
  3. Green AppleCrisp, tart, clean.
  4. GrapeJuicy, wine-like, soft or tangy.
Part IV

Where Acidity Comes From

Acidity can be shaped by the plant, the place, the process, the roast, and the brew.

  1. Origin and VarietySome coffees naturally carry more brightness.
  2. ProcessingWashed, natural, honey, and fermented coffees can express acidity differently.
  3. RoastingLighter roasts often show more acidity. Darker roasts usually reduce it.
  4. BrewingUnder-extraction can make acidity feel sour and thin.
Part V

How to Notice Acidity

Do not begin by deciding whether acidity is good or bad.

Ask what kind of acidity it is.

  1. Is It Sharp?Pointed, lemon-like, cutting.
  2. Is It Juicy?Fruit-like, mouth-watering, pleasant.
  3. Is It Clean?Bright but not harsh.
  4. Is It Thin?Sour without enough sweetness or body.

Page Direction

Acidity is a kind of lift.

It becomes enjoyable when sweetness, aroma, and body give it somewhere to belong.
