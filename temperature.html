Temperature

The same coffee does not taste exactly the same throughout the cup.

This surprises many people when they begin paying attention to coffee. A coffee that seemed bitter at first may later feel sweeter. A coffee that seemed simple may later reveal more detail.
Part I

Why Temperature Matters

Coffee is not a fixed object in the cup.

As it cools, aroma changes, taste changes, texture changes, and our perception changes with them.

  1. MovementThe cup changes while we drink it.
  2. RangeDifferent temperatures reveal different things.
  3. PatienceA single sip rarely tells the whole story.
Part II

The First Hot Sip

When coffee is very hot, temperature dominates attention. The mouth notices heat before it notices many smaller details.

  1. Aroma Rises QuicklySteam carries volatile aromas from the cup.
  2. Bitterness May Stand ForwardIt can feel stronger in the first hot sips.
  3. Sweetness May Be Harder To ReadHeat can make sweetness less obvious at first.
  4. Fine Details May WaitExperienced tasters rarely judge only from the first sip.
Part III

As The Cup Cools

As temperature falls, many coffees become easier to read.

The cup may feel more balanced. Sweetness, acidity, texture, and aftertaste may become clearer.

  1. SweetnessOften becomes easier to find.
  2. AcidityMay become easier to describe.
  3. BodyWeight and texture become easier to evaluate.
  4. RelationshipsHow sweetness, acidity, bitterness, and aroma fit together becomes clearer.
Part IV

Cooler Coffee

A cooler cup can reveal things that were difficult to notice earlier. It does not tell a truer story. It tells a different part of the same story.

  1. FruitFruit impressions may become more obvious.
  2. Floral NotesDelicate aromas may be easier to recognise.
  3. DefectsStaleness, harshness, sourness, or rough extraction may become more visible.
  4. FinishWhat remains after swallowing may be easier to judge.
Part V

A Useful Habit

When evaluating a coffee, do not rush.

Drink it through its temperature range. Taste it when hot. Taste it again when warm. Taste it once more when cool.

  1. What Appears First?Heat, aroma, bitterness, strength, comfort.
  2. What Opens Later?Sweetness, acidity, fruit, texture, balance.
  3. What Remains?Aftertaste, dryness, sweetness, clarity, roughness.

Page Direction

Temperature is not separate from flavour.

It helps shape what we perceive. A coffee is not one experience. It is a sequence of experiences unfolding through time.
