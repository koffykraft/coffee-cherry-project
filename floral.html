Floral

Floral notes are usually smelled before they are tasted.

Floral does not mean the coffee contains flowers. It means the aroma reminds the drinker of flowers, perfume, blossom, tea, or delicate plant fragrance.
Part I

Where Floral Lives

Floral impressions are usually carried by aroma.

They may appear in the dry fragrance, the wet aroma, the steam from the cup, or the retronasal aroma while drinking.

  1. Dry FragranceGround coffee may show delicate perfume before water is added.
  2. Wet AromaHot water can release more visible floral notes.
  3. Retronasal AromaWhile drinking, aroma rises from the mouth toward the nose.
  4. FinishSome floral impressions remain softly after swallowing.
Part II

Common Floral Directions

Floral is a family, not one flavour. Different coffees may suggest different kinds of floral character.

  1. JasmineLight, sweet, high, tea-like perfume.
  2. RoseSoft, rounded, sweet, sometimes fruit-linked.
  3. Orange BlossomFloral with citrus freshness.
  4. ChamomileGentle, herbal, sweet, tea-like.
  5. General BlossomA broad fresh-flower impression without a precise name.
Part III

Why Floral Can Be Hard To Catch

Floral notes are often delicate.

They may disappear under heat, roast character, strong fermentation, heavy body, bitterness, or distraction.

  1. TemperatureVery hot coffee may hide fine aromatic detail.
  2. RoastDarker roast character can cover delicate floral aroma.
  3. ProcessingStrong fruit or fermentation notes may become louder than floral notes.
  4. MemoryA drinker must have some remembered floral reference to name it.
Part IV

Where It May Come From

Floral expression can be influenced by variety, environment, processing, roast, and brewing. No single cause should be assumed too quickly.

  1. VarietySome varieties are more often associated with floral aroma.
  2. EnvironmentGrowing conditions affect the material available before roasting.
  3. ProcessingCareful processing can preserve or reveal delicate aromatics.
  4. RoastingRoast choices can protect, mute, or overwhelm floral character.
  5. BrewingExtraction and serving temperature affect whether floral notes are noticed.
Part V

A Professional Reading

Do not force floral language.

Begin with the broad signal. Is the coffee fragrant, perfumed, blossom-like, tea-like, or simply delicate?

  1. Is It Aromatic?Does the note come through the nose more than the tongue?
  2. Is It Fresh Or Dried?Fresh blossom and dried flower are different impressions.
  3. Is It Supported?Does sweetness, acidity, and clarity give the floral note space?
  4. Is It Stable?Does it remain as the cup cools, or vanish quickly?

Page Direction

Floral is an aromatic impression, not a performance word.

The task is to notice delicacy without exaggerating it. Sometimes floral is clear. Sometimes it is only a quiet lift in the aroma.
