Sweetness

Coffee can feel sweet even when no sugar is added.

Sweetness in coffee is often a perception. It may come from taste, aroma, balance, roast, texture, or memory.
Part I

Not Only Sugar

A sweet coffee is not the same as a sugary coffee.

Sometimes sweetness is felt as softness. Sometimes as fruit. Sometimes as caramel, chocolate, honey, or ripe grain.

  1. Taste SweetnessA direct sweet signal on the tongue.
  2. Aroma SweetnessCaramel, fruit, vanilla, chocolate, honey-like impressions.
  3. Balance SweetnessWhen acidity and bitterness are held in a pleasant shape.
Part II

Sweetness Softens

Sweetness makes other parts of the cup easier to receive.

  1. With AcidityIt can turn sharpness into fruit-like brightness.
  2. With BitternessIt can make cocoa or roast feel pleasant instead of harsh.
  3. With BodyIt can make coffee feel round, smooth, or syrupy.
Part III

Where Sweet Impressions Appear

Sweetness can appear in many forms.

Some are light and fruit-like. Some are darker and roast-like.

  1. Fruit SweetnessBerry, peach, mango, grape, dried fruit.
  2. Brown SweetnessJaggery, caramel, toffee, molasses, brown sugar.
  3. Soft SweetnessMilk chocolate, cream, honey, ripe banana, cooked grain.
Part IV

What Can Hide Sweetness?

A coffee may contain sweet impressions, but still not feel sweet. Other signals can cover them.

  1. Too Much BitternessCan make the cup feel hard or tiring.
  2. Sharp AcidityCan make the cup feel sour before sweetness is noticed.
  3. Thin BodyCan make sweetness feel weak or unsupported.
  4. Very Hot CoffeeCan make sweetness harder to read at first.
Part V

How to Notice Sweetness

Do not look only for sugar.

Ask whether the cup becomes softer, rounder, or more pleasant as it cools.

  1. Look for RoundnessDoes the coffee feel softer than expected?
  2. Look for FruitDoes acidity feel ripe rather than sharp?
  3. Look for FinishDoes anything sweet remain after swallowing?

Page Direction

Sweetness is often the cup becoming kind.

It may not shout. Sometimes it simply makes the rest of the coffee easier to love.
