Storage

Coffee can look finished long before it is stable.

After drying, coffee still needs protection. Moisture, heat, oxygen, odour, insects, and time can all change the material before roasting.
Part I

After Drying

Dry coffee is not inactive.

It is quieter, but it still responds to storage conditions. This is true for parchment coffee, green coffee, and roasted coffee in different ways.

  1. Parchment CoffeeCoffee may rest with the parchment layer still protecting the seed.
  2. Green CoffeeAfter hulling, the seed is more exposed to storage conditions.
  3. Roasted CoffeeAfter roasting, aroma loss and staling become faster concerns.
Part II

What Storage Must Protect Against

Storage is not only about keeping coffee in a bag. It is about reducing damage while the coffee waits.

  1. MoistureToo much moisture increases risk of mould, instability, and ageing problems.
  2. HeatWarm storage can speed deterioration.
  3. OxygenExposure can contribute to fading and stale flavours.
  4. OdoursCoffee can absorb unwanted smells from its surroundings.
  5. PestsInsects and rodents can damage stored coffee.
Part III

Green Coffee Changes Slowly

Green coffee can remain useful for months.

But it does not remain exactly the same. Fresh crop character can fade. Age can appear. Poor storage can make decline faster.

  1. FreshnessNewer green coffee may show clearer acidity, sweetness, and aroma potential.
  2. FadingWith time, some brightness and aromatic clarity may reduce.
  3. Baggy NotesOlder or poorly stored coffee may develop dull, papery, woody, or jute-like impressions.
  4. Uneven LotsMixed moisture or mixed quality can age unpredictably.
Part IV

Packaging And Place

Storage depends on both container and environment. Good packaging cannot fully repair a poor warehouse. A good room still needs suitable protection.

  1. BagsJute, GrainPro-style liners, vacuum packs, and other systems protect differently.
  2. WarehouseClean, dry, cool, and odour-free storage matters.
  3. StackingCoffee should be protected from floor moisture and poor airflow.
  4. MovementTransport exposes coffee to heat, humidity, handling, and delay.
Part V

What Reaches The Cup

Good storage rarely announces itself.

Poor storage often does. It may show as faded sweetness, dull aroma, woody age, mustiness, or a tired finish.

  1. ClarityGood storage helps preserve what the coffee had.
  2. Roasting BehaviourMoisture and age affect how the coffee responds to heat.
  3. Shelf LifeBetter storage gives the roaster more reliable material.
  4. LossStorage cannot improve coffee indefinitely. Its first job is protection.

Page Direction

Storage is protection through time.

It does not create quality by itself. It helps preserve quality long enough for roasting and brewing to reveal it.
