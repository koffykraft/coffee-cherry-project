Bitterness

Bitterness belongs to coffee. The question is how it appears.

Not all bitterness is bad. Some bitterness gives structure. Some bitterness gives pleasure. Some bitterness becomes harsh.
Part I

A Familiar Signal

Most people expect coffee to be at least a little bitter.

That expectation is not wrong. Bitterness is part of coffee’s identity.

  1. Gentle BitternessCocoa, tea, roast, almond skin, dark caramel.
  2. Strong BitternessDark chocolate, heavy roast, intense espresso.
  3. Harsh BitternessBurnt, medicinal, metallic, tiring, rough.
Part II

Bitterness Gives Depth

A cup with no bitterness can feel empty. A cup with too much bitterness can feel closed.

  1. In BalanceBitterness can make coffee feel complete.
  2. With SweetnessIt may become cocoa, caramel, roasted nut, or dark chocolate.
  3. Without SupportIt may become sharp, dry, hollow, or unpleasant.
Part III

Where It Can Come From

Bitterness does not have one source.

Plant chemistry, roasting, brewing, and serving temperature can all change how it feels.

  1. Coffee ItselfCaffeine and other bitter compounds are naturally present.
  2. RoastingDarker roasting often increases roast bitterness.
  3. BrewingOver-extraction can pull more drying and bitter impressions.
  4. TemperatureVery hot coffee can make bitterness feel stronger at first.
Part IV

Bitterness and Roast

Roast changes the kind of bitterness people notice.

  1. Light RoastBitterness may be lighter, with more acidity visible.
  2. Medium RoastBitterness may sit with sweetness and body.
  3. Dark RoastBitterness may become more dominant, smoky, or char-like.
Part V

How to Read Bitterness

Do not ask only whether the coffee is bitter.

Ask what kind of bitter it is.

  1. Is It Pleasant?Cocoa, tea, roast, dark caramel.
  2. Is It Drying?Does it pull moisture from the mouth?
  3. Is It Burnt?Smoke, ash, char, over-roast.
  4. Does It Stay Too Long?A finish can be pleasant, or it can become tiring.

Page Direction

Bitterness is not automatically a defect.

It becomes useful when it gives coffee depth without taking over the whole cup.
