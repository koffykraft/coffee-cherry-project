Roast

Roast notes describe the sensory presence of fire, browning, and heat within the cup.

This page describes roast as a sensory experience, not roasting as a production process.

A coffee may be roasted and still show little roast character. Another may be dominated by roast.
Part I

The Shape Of Roast

Roast often shifts attention away from origin and toward transformation.

As roast character increases, browning, toast, smoke, carbon, and heat-derived flavours become more visible.

  1. BrowningThe sensory signature of heat acting on organic material.
  2. WarmthRoast often creates familiar and comforting impressions.
  3. DominanceStrong roast character can overwhelm other flavours.
  4. StructureRoast influences bitterness, texture, and finish.
Part II

Common Roast Directions

Roast is not a single flavour.

  1. ToastLight browning and grain-like warmth.
  2. Roasted NutHeat-developed nutty character.
  3. SmokeCombustion-like aromatic influence.
  4. CharDarker and more aggressive than toast.
  5. AshThe residue of extreme roast influence.
Part III

Supporting Or Dominating?

Roast can support a coffee or replace it.

The key question is whether roast contributes to expression or hides it.

  1. Supporting RoastWorks with fruit, sweetness, and structure.
  2. Dominant RoastBecomes the main sensory message.
  3. Integrated RoastFeels connected to the whole cup.
  4. Excessive RoastFlattens complexity and reduces clarity.
Part IV

Where Roast Character Appears

Roast notes may appear across aroma, taste, body, texture, and finish.

  1. AromaToast, smoke, cocoa, and browning aromas.
  2. TasteBitterness and caramelised impressions.
  3. TextureRoast can change perceived weight, dryness, and mouthfeel.
  4. FinishRoast may persist long after swallowing.
Part V

A Professional Reading

Do not assume roast is either good or bad.

Instead ask how much roast is present and what it contributes.

  1. How Visible?Subtle, moderate, or dominant?
  2. What Kind?Toast, smoke, char, ash, cocoa, or caramel?
  3. What Is Hidden?Has roast obscured other qualities?
  4. Is It Appropriate?Does the roast suit the coffee and intended experience?

Page Direction

Roast is the sensory language of transformation by fire.

It reminds the taster that coffee is not only an agricultural product but also a cooked product, shaped profoundly by heat before it reaches the cup.
