Coffee Leaf

The coffee leaf is one of the most visible parts of the coffee plant, yet one of the least explored.

Most people encounter coffee through the roasted seed. Yet the plant spends far more of its life producing, maintaining, and renewing leaves than producing fruit.
Part I

The Leaf As A Living Organ

Leaves are not passive structures. They capture light, regulate water, exchange gases, and support the growth of the entire plant.

  1. PhotosynthesisLeaves convert sunlight into usable plant energy.
  2. GrowthLeaves support the development of roots, branches, flowers, and fruit.
  3. ProtectionLeaves participate in defence against environmental stress.
  4. RenewalThe canopy is continuously changing through growth and ageing.
Part II

Not All Leaves Are The Same

A coffee tree carries leaves of different ages, positions, exposures, and physiological states.

  1. Young LeavesOften softer, lighter, and chemically distinct from mature leaves.
  2. Mature LeavesThe dominant working leaves of the canopy.
  3. Sun LeavesAdapted to higher light exposure.
  4. Shade LeavesAdapted to lower light conditions.
Part III

The Leaf Beyond Agriculture

In several coffee-growing regions, leaves have long been used as beverages, household preparations, or traditional foods.

  1. EthiopiaTraditional preparations include leaf-based beverages alongside coffee seed use.
  2. IndonesiaCoffee leaf infusions remain known in parts of Sumatra.
  3. Local KnowledgeMany practices existed before modern specialty coffee emerged.
  4. RediscoveryInterest in coffee leaves is growing again through research and culinary exploration.
Part IV

A Different Expression Of The Same Plant

The leaf is not a substitute for roasted coffee. It is a different expression of the same species.

  1. Shared OriginLeaf and seed belong to the same plant.
  2. Different MaterialsThe compounds present and their proportions are not identical.
  3. Different PreparationLeaves are typically infused or decocted rather than roasted and brewed as coffee.
  4. Different ExperienceThe resulting beverage occupies a different sensory territory.
Part V

A Professional Reading

The leaf deserves to be studied on its own terms rather than judged only against roasted coffee.

  1. What Leaf?Age, position, season, and health all matter.
  2. How Processed?Fresh, dried, heated, oxidised, or otherwise transformed.
  3. How Prepared?Infusion and decoction create different outcomes.
  4. What Can It Become?The leaf remains an open field for research, tradition, and innovation.

Page Direction

The coffee leaf is not outside the coffee story.

It has always been part of the plant. What is changing is our willingness to look at it directly rather than only through the lens of the roasted seed.
