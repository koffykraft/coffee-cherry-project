Taste

Taste is smaller than flavour. That makes it easier to understand.

When people say coffee tastes like chocolate, mango, jasmine, or smoke, they are usually speaking about flavour. Taste itself is more basic.
Part I

What the Tongue Notices

The tongue does not identify blueberry.

It notices broad signals. Those signals help shape the cup, but they do not carry the whole experience.

  1. SweetSoftening, rounded, pleasant, sometimes syrup-like.
  2. SourSharp, bright, lively, sometimes clean, sometimes harsh.
  3. BitterFamiliar in coffee. Pleasant in balance, rough when excessive.
  4. SaltyUsually quiet in coffee, but sometimes part of balance.
  5. UmamiSavoury depth. Not common as a main coffee word, but useful to know.
Part II

Taste Gives Structure

Taste is not decoration. It gives the cup its basic shape.

  1. Sweetness SoftensIt can make acidity gentler and bitterness more pleasant.
  2. Acidity LiftsIt can make coffee feel alive, fresh, or sharp.
  3. Bitterness AnchorsIt can give depth, seriousness, or too much weight.
Part III

Taste and Aroma Work Together

A sweet taste with a fruity aroma may feel like ripe fruit.

A bitter taste with roasted aroma may feel like dark chocolate.

The tongue gives part of the message. The nose completes much of it.

  1. Sweet + Fruit AromaMay become peach, berry, mango, or dried fruit in the mind.
  2. Bitter + Roast AromaMay become cocoa, toast, roasted nuts, or smoke.
  3. Sour + Citrus AromaMay become lemon, orange, lime, or grapefruit.
Part IV

Balance Matters

The same taste can be welcome or unpleasant depending on balance.

  1. Good SournessFresh, lively, fruit-like.
  2. Rough SournessSharp, thin, aggressive.
  3. Good BitternessCocoa, tea, roast, structure.
  4. Rough BitternessHarsh, burnt, medicinal, tiring.
Part V

A Simple Practice

Taste a coffee hot. Then again when it is warm. Then again later.

Do not look for notes first. Look for changes in sweetness, sourness, and bitterness.

  1. HotHeat can hide sweetness and increase general intensity.
  2. WarmBalance often becomes easier to read.
  3. CoolAcidity and sweetness may become more visible.

Page Direction

Taste is the cup’s basic grammar.

It does not tell the whole story, but without it the story has no structure.
